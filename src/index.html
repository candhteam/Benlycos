Solving the last mile connectivity challenge

The adoption of technology is slow in developing countries across the world and the cost associated with such adoption is one of the major challenges that slow down mass adoption.

Switch your internet speed to the max!

After considerable research and analysis, the Benlycos team decided to focus on last-mile internet connectivity as the first problem to make an impact. The decision was made by taking into consideration a wide range of facts including the overall impact on people’s lives, the list of industries that will benefit from a solution to this problem, and the viability of a solution that can have the reach and scale to make business sense to all parties involved.

Solution

The solution derives its approach from the age-old wisdom credited to ‘Tecumseh’ - “A single twig breaks, but the bundle of twigs is strong”. Drawing parallels, every wireless last mile connection is relatively unreliable due to various factors that influence the quality of data transmission. Thus by combining multiple such unreliable sources of connectivity and combining them at L3 of the OSI model, we are able to increase the speed, reliability, and security of the communication at the last mile of Internet usage.

We validated the assumption of the effectiveness of such a solution by launching an MVP of our product under the brand name “Zifilink” which was sold to more than 200 customers over a period of 3 years.

ONGOING PROJECT

Building the next generation solution for internet connectivity

Based on the learning and experience we could gather by serving the customers using ‘Zifilink’ for the last 3 years, we decided to build the next generation of connectivity solutions under the brand name ‘Bloom’ to provide a more enhanced experience to the customers thereby cementing our commitment to solving the last mile connectivity challenge for the businesses and people who need that the most.

What’s new in the BLOOM

Due to the vast range of end applications customers were putting the product to, there was a need to enhance the software stack to accommodate support for various protocols and aggregation mechanisms which will gel well with other applications that the end users need to work together with Bloom. This meant a total overhaul of the system was needed and equally lengthy testing cycles needed to be executed to confirm compatibility.

One of the other challenges was the lack of 4G/5G signal strength which caused performance degradation for datalinks based on the location of the device. We had to provision for the usage of antennas as an accessory to solve this challenge and enhance the user experience. This proved to be a very useful arrangement in the future as this provided us with more advanced functionality to work with available networks to improve performance at a more granular level.

By integrating the 5G/4G modems into the product enclosure, we have also been able to make the product more sturdy and resilient to be used in moving vehicles for a longer period of time without any degradation in performance which was a great advantage.

By building the Bloom product completely in-house, we were able to have a 100% ‘make in India’ product which now can be not only manufactured and sold based on sales forecast but gives us much more control over the build quality and delivery timelines of the product..

The Bloom product is planned to be launched at Gitex 2022 in October in Dubai for the Middle East and African market and in Mumbai during Broadcast India show 2022 in the same week for the Indian broadcast market.
